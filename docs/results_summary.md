# Results Summary

## Overview

The LDA results revealed **clear and consistent differences** between Test and T20 cricket commentary, supporting the initial hypothesis.

---

## T20 Commentary Findings

Key characteristics:
- Strong emphasis on:
  - Runs
  - Sixes
  - Aggressive batting
- Frequent use of filler words:
  - "oh", "uh", "like", "um"
- Minimal use of technical cricket terminology
- Limited references to pitch behavior or bowling mechanics

Interpretation:
The fast-paced nature of T20 leaves little time for structured analysis, encouraging spontaneous and entertainment-driven commentary.

Unexpected observation:
- Overrepresentation of the word *"Zimbabwe"* in some topics despite limited match appearances, indicating dataset imbalance.

---

## Test Commentary Findings

Key characteristics:
- Strong emphasis on:
  - Bowling techniques
  - Seam movement
  - Pitch conditions
- Structured, analytical language
- Near absence of filler words

Interpretation:
The slower pace of Test cricket allows commentators to articulate deeper tactical insights and technical explanations.

Unexpected observation:
- One topic contained unrelated words (e.g., "war", "bank", "tongue"), suggesting occasional off-topic discussion.

---

## Comparative Insight

| Dimension | T20 | Test |
|--------|-----|------|
| Technical vocabulary | Low | High |
| Filler words | High | Minimal |
| Narrative depth | Reactive | Analytical |
| Commentary pacing | Spontaneous | Structured |

Overall, Test commentary demonstrates **greater linguistic complexity**, while T20 commentary prioritizes accessibility and immediacy.
